# SUMMARY REPORT: “CD AWS”

## Event Details

- **Date:** Saturday, May 30, 2026
- **Time:** 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM
- **Location:** Saigon Campus - Level 26, Bitexco Financial Tower
- **Event Type:** Community Sharing and Knowledge Exchange
- **Role:** Attendee

## Event Objectives

The CD AWS event was organized to create an environment where members of the First Cloud Journey community could exchange knowledge, experiences, and practical skills.

The main objectives of the event included:

- Introducing useful platforms for learning and practicing AWS services.
- Sharing practical experiences from participating in Hackathon competitions.
- Helping students improve their confidence and communication skills.
- Introducing real-world technology projects developed by community members.
- Raising awareness about procrastination and effective time management.
- Creating opportunities for students to connect with other members of the AWS community.

## Agenda Highlights

### Welcome and Event Introduction (9:00 AM – 9:10 AM)

The event began with a short introduction to the program, speakers, and main activities. Participants were welcomed to the AWS Vietnam Office and given an overview of the five sharing sessions.

The opening also provided an opportunity for participants to meet other members of the First Cloud Journey community and prepare for the upcoming presentations.

---

### Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci (9:10 AM – 9:40 AM)

**Speaker:** Huỳnh Thái Linh

The first presentation introduced **AWS Cloud Quest** and **Floci**, two useful platforms that support learners in developing their AWS knowledge and practical cloud skills.

The speaker explained how AWS Cloud Quest uses interactive and game-based scenarios to help learners explore AWS services. Instead of learning only through theoretical documents, users can complete practical missions and solve cloud-related problems in a simulated environment.

Floci was also introduced as a supporting platform for organizing learning activities and improving the cloud learning experience.

The main topics included:

- Introduction to AWS Cloud Quest.
- Game-based learning for AWS services.
- Practical cloud scenarios and interactive exercises.
- Using Floci to support the AWS learning process.
- Building a consistent and effective cloud learning roadmap.
- Combining theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice.

This session helped me understand that learning cloud computing becomes more effective when theoretical knowledge is combined with practical exercises. AWS Cloud Quest can also make the learning process more interesting and easier to follow.

---

### Hackathon – “HA! A Tons of Fun” (9:40 AM – 10:10 AM)

**Team:** The Ballers  
**Presenters:** Huỳnh An Khương, Mai Quốc Anh, and Nguyễn Trần Minh Quân

The second session focused on the team's experience participating in a Hackathon. The presenters shared their journey from generating an initial idea to developing, debugging, and presenting their final solution.

They discussed the challenges of working under time pressure, dividing tasks among team members, fixing unexpected technical problems, and ensuring that the project could be completed before the deadline.

The main topics included:

- Generating and evaluating project ideas.
- Assigning responsibilities based on each member’s strengths.
- Working effectively under limited time.
- Debugging software and resolving technical issues.
- Communicating continuously during the development process.
- Preparing and presenting the final Hackathon product.
- Learning from mistakes and unexpected situations.

Through this session, I learned that a Hackathon is not only a programming competition but also an opportunity to improve teamwork, communication, problem-solving, and presentation skills.

A successful team needs to manage time carefully, communicate clearly, and remain flexible when problems occur.

---

### Why We Always Need Confidence (10:10 AM – 10:40 AM)

**Speaker:** Nguyễn Quỳnh Như

The third session focused on the importance of confidence in university life, communication, presentations, interviews, and future careers.

The speaker explained that confidence is not an ability that people naturally have from the beginning. It can be developed gradually through preparation, practice, experience, and a willingness to step outside one’s comfort zone.

The session also showed how a lack of confidence can prevent students from expressing ideas, asking questions, participating in activities, and taking advantage of valuable opportunities.

The main topics included:

- The importance of confidence in study and work.
- Overcoming fear when speaking in front of others.
- Preparing carefully before presentations.
- Expressing opinions clearly and respectfully.
- Learning from mistakes instead of being afraid of failure.
- Building confidence through small and consistent actions.
- Developing communication and leadership skills.

From this session, I learned that confidence has an important influence on both personal development and professional opportunities.

To become more confident, I need to actively participate in discussions, ask questions, practice presenting, and accept mistakes as part of the learning process.

---

### Tử Vi Đại Việt Project Sharing (10:40 AM – 11:10 AM)

The fourth session introduced the **Tử Vi Đại Việt** project, a technology product that applies software development to the field of Vietnamese astrology.

The presenter demonstrated the project interface and explained the main idea behind the system. The session also provided an overview of how an initial idea could be transformed into a functional web-based product.

The main topics included:

- Identifying the purpose and target users of the application.
- Planning the main features of the system.
- Designing a clear and user-friendly interface.
- Developing a web application based on user requirements.
- Testing and improving the product.
- Presenting the finished project to users.
- Connecting traditional content with modern technology.

This session helped me better understand the process of building a real software product, from identifying an idea to designing, developing, and presenting the final application.

I also learned that a good project must not only function correctly but also provide a convenient and understandable experience for users.

---

### A Familiar Enemy – Procrastination (11:10 AM – 11:50 AM)

The final presentation discussed **procrastination**, a common problem among students and employees.

The speaker described procrastination as a familiar enemy that can negatively affect productivity, learning progress, deadlines, and personal goals. The session explained several reasons why people delay important tasks, such as fear of failure, lack of motivation, unclear goals, distractions, and poor time management.

The main topics included:

- Common signs of procrastination.
- Reasons people delay important tasks.
- The effects of procrastination on study and work.
- Dividing large tasks into smaller steps.
- Setting clear and achievable deadlines.
- Prioritizing important tasks.
- Reducing distractions from social media and entertainment.
- Building consistent and productive habits.

From this session, I learned that procrastination cannot be solved only by waiting for motivation. It is necessary to begin with small actions, create a clear plan, and complete tasks step by step.

Managing time effectively and maintaining discipline can help improve both productivity and work quality.

---

### Closing and Networking (11:50 AM – 12:00 PM)

At the end of the event, the organizers summarized the main topics and thanked the speakers and participants.

Participants also had an opportunity to communicate with the speakers, exchange experiences, take photographs, and connect with other members of the First Cloud Journey community.

## Key Takeaways

### AWS Learning and Hands-on Practice

- AWS Cloud Quest provides an interactive way to practice AWS services.
- Game-based learning can make technical knowledge easier to understand.
- Hands-on practice is necessary to strengthen theoretical knowledge.
- A clear learning roadmap helps learners maintain consistent progress.

### Teamwork and Hackathon Experience

- Effective communication is essential when working under time pressure.
- Tasks should be assigned according to the strengths of each team member.
- Hackathons improve programming, teamwork, problem-solving, and presentation skills.
- Technical problems should be handled calmly and systematically.
- A successful project requires both technical knowledge and effective collaboration.

### Confidence and Communication

- Confidence can be developed through preparation and regular practice.
- Students should actively express their opinions and ask questions.
- Mistakes are part of the learning and personal development process.
- Communication and public-speaking skills are important for future careers.
- Leaving one’s comfort zone can create new learning opportunities.

### Real-world Project Development

- A successful software project begins with a clear idea and user requirements.
- User interface and user experience are important parts of application development.
- Testing and continuous improvement help increase product quality.
- Technology can be applied creatively to many different fields.
- Presenting a project clearly is as important as developing its functions.

### Productivity and Time Management

- Procrastination can reduce work quality and delay important goals.
- Large tasks should be divided into smaller and more manageable steps.
- Clear deadlines help maintain focus and responsibility.
- Distractions should be reduced during study and work.
- Discipline and consistent action are more reliable than temporary motivation.

## Event Experience

Attending the CD AWS event was a valuable experience because the program combined technical knowledge, practical project experience, and personal development skills.

The event did not focus only on AWS services. It also addressed several important topics for students, including teamwork, confidence, communication, project development, productivity, and time management.

The speakers shared personal experiences instead of presenting only theoretical information. Their stories made the sessions more relatable and helped participants understand how knowledge and skills can be applied in real situations.

The Hackathon session showed the importance of teamwork and adaptability, while the confidence session encouraged students to become more active in communication and presentation activities.

### Practical Technical Knowledge

The introduction to AWS Cloud Quest and Floci gave me additional resources for continuing my cloud learning journey.

The Tử Vi Đại Việt project also helped me understand how a software idea can be developed into a real application through planning, interface design, implementation, and testing.

### Personal Development

The sessions about confidence and procrastination were especially useful for improving my study habits.

I realized that technical skills alone are not enough. Students also need confidence, communication skills, discipline, and effective time management to work successfully in professional environments.

### Networking Opportunities

The event created an opportunity to meet other students, speakers, and members of the First Cloud Journey community.

Through these interactions, I gained more motivation to participate in community activities, improve my AWS skills, and continue developing both technical and soft skills.

## Lessons Learned

After attending the event, I learned several important lessons:

- Cloud computing should be learned through both theory and hands-on practice.
- Interactive platforms can make the AWS learning process more effective.
- Teamwork and communication are essential in software projects.
- Confidence can be improved through preparation and repeated practice.
- Real-world projects require careful planning and understanding of users.

- Community events provide valuable opportunities for knowledge sharing and professional networking.
